Oklahoma Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, Oklahoma offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, income tax, education, employment, vehicle, recreation, veterans home, financial. Here's what Oklahoma veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. 100% Disabled Veteran Homestead Property Tax Exemption100% P&T
    Full exemption (100% of fair cash value of homestead)

    A head of household honorably discharged from the Armed Forces or Oklahoma National Guard and certified by the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs as 100% permanently and totally disabled (service-connected) is exempt from ad valorem (property) tax on the FULL fair cash value of their homestead. The surviving spouse (if not remarried) also qualifies. Must own and occupy the homestead as of January 1. Applied via OTC Form 998 / Form 538-H with a current USDVA award letter.

Income Tax

  1. Active Duty Military Pay DeductionAll veterans
    100% of active-duty military pay deductible

    100% of income received as salary or compensation by an Oklahoma resident serving in any component of the U.S. Armed Forces is deductible from Oklahoma taxable income. (Separately, VA disability compensation is not taxable federally and is therefore not included in Oklahoma taxable income; the state pages do not address VA disability comp as a distinct line item, but it is excluded by virtue of not being part of federal AGI.)

    Official details ↗
  2. Military Retirement Pay Income Tax Exemption (100%)All veterans
    Military retirement fully tax-exempt (100%, effective TY2022)

    Beginning Tax Year 2022, Oklahoma residents may exclude 100% of retirement benefits received from any component of the U.S. Armed Forces from state income tax. This is a full exemption that replaced the older partial exclusion (greater of $10,000 or 75%). Confirmed by the Oklahoma Tax Commission income-tax help page.

    Official details ↗

Education

  1. Heroes Promise Scholarship (Children of KIA Service Members)Combat service
    Tuition scholarship up to 48 months (in-state tuition)

    Created by HB 1343 (effective July 1, 2011), provides an in-state tuition scholarship/waiver to children of Oklahoma military personnel killed in action after January 1, 2000, for up to 48 months. Recipients are exempt from Oklahoma's Promise high-school academic and family-income requirements. Administered by the Oklahoma State Regents for Higher Education.

    Official details ↗

Employment

  1. State Employment Veterans PreferenceAll veterans
    5-point preference (10-point for disabled); absolute preference and exam waiver at 30%+

    Honorably discharged veterans receive 5 preference points added to state employment exam scores; war veterans with a service-connected disability receive 10 points. 'Absolute Preference' veterans (30%+ disability rating) are placed at the top of certified hiring lists, and special disabled veterans (30%+) may bypass entrance exams. Direct-hire authority exists for nursing positions.

Vehicle

  1. Disabled American Veteran (DAV) License Plate50%+ rating
    $5 registration (plus insurance/mailing fees); requires 50%+ service-connected disability

    Veterans certified by the USDVA with a service-connected disability of at least 50% qualify for a Disabled American Veteran (DAV) license plate at a reduced registration rate of $5 plus applicable insurance and mailing fees. Applied via Service Oklahoma Form 751-J.

    Official details ↗
  2. Free Driver License Issuance/Renewal for 100% Disabled Veterans100% P&T
    Free driver license issuance/renewal

    An honorably discharged veteran certified by the USDVA as a disabled veteran in receipt of compensation at the 100% rate for a permanent disability is not charged a fee for the issuance or renewal of an Oklahoma driver license.

Recreation

  1. Free State Park and Museum AdmissionAll veterans
    Free admission to state parks and museums

    All honorably discharged veterans who are current Oklahoma residents receive free admission to all state-owned or state-operated parks and museums. Documentation required: valid Oklahoma driver license or state photo ID plus DD-214 or other honorable discharge evidence. No disability rating required.

    Official details ↗
  2. Hunting/Fishing License Exemption for Disabled Veterans60%+ rating
    Free license at 60%+; $25 lifetime combo at 60%+; $200 lifetime combo under 60%

    Veterans with a 60% or greater service-connected disability are exempt from hunting and fishing license fees (free permit), or may purchase a Disability Lifetime Combination hunting/fishing license for $25. Veterans with LESS than 60% disability may purchase the Disability Lifetime Combination license for $200. 100% disabled veterans also receive fee exemptions for deer gun/archery/primitive firearms/bonus deer licenses.

    Official details ↗

Veterans Home

  1. Oklahoma State Veterans Homes / CentersAll veterans
    Full cost of care free for 70%+ service-connected veterans; otherwise per-diem/income-based

    ODVA operates state veterans homes (locations include Ardmore, Claremore, Clinton, Lawton, Norman, Sulphur, and Sallisaw) providing long-term skilled nursing care, federally approved by USDVA for per diem and licensed by the Oklahoma State Department of Health. Eligibility requires USDVA certification for per diem, at least 90 days active-duty service (exceptions for service-connected discharge), and a disability requiring intermediate/skilled nursing care. Admission is prioritized by oldest wartime period. Veterans with a 70% or greater service-connected rating receive FULL cost of care at no charge (effective 06-01-2009).

    Official details ↗

Financial

  1. Sales Tax Exemption for 100% Disabled Veterans100% P&T
    Sales tax exempt up to $25,000/year of purchases ($1,000/year for surviving spouse)

    Oklahoma residents certified by the USDVA as receiving service-connected compensation at the 100% rate for a permanent disability may obtain a sales tax exemption permit on purchases of tangible personal property and services. The exemption is capped at $25,000 of qualifying sales per year. A surviving spouse (not remarried) is capped at $1,000 per year.

    Official details ↗
