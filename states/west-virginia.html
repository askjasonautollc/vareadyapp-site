West Virginia Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, West Virginia offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, income tax, education, employment, vehicle, recreation, veterans home. Here's what West Virginia veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. Disabled Veteran Homestead Property Tax Exemption100% P&T
    Exemption from certain property taxes

    Veterans rated 100% permanent and total (P&T) disabled by the VA may be exempt from certain ad valorem property taxes on their homestead. Administered through the county assessor under W. Va. Code §11-6B. The WV Veterans Affairs page states the requirement as '100 percent permanent and disabled by the federal Department of Veterans Affairs.' — VERIFIED: The current (2024+) West Virginia disabled-veteran property benefit is the Disabled Veteran Real Property Tax Credit, NOT the old 100% P&T flat homestead exemption that the research leads with. Per WV Tax Division publication TSD 455 (Rev. January 2026): honorably discharged veterans considered 90-100% permanently and totally (P&T) disabled by the VA may claim a credit equal to the ACTUAL real property tax paid on a homestead used/occupied exclusively for residential purposes (used or occupied at least 6 months). The credit is claimed on Form DV-1 / IT-140 Line 21(B); property taxes must be paid timely (first half before Oct 1, second half before Apr 1). Threshold is 90-100% P&T (P&T required), and the extent is the full property tax actually paid (a refundable income-tax credit), not a $20,000 partial assessed-value exemption. The research's second property item (the 90% credit) is essentially correct; its first item (100% P&T flat homestead exemption as the headline benefit) is outdated/superseded for veterans as of Jan 1, 2024.

  2. Disabled Veteran Real Property Tax Credit (Income Tax Credit)90%+ rating
    Credit equal to property tax paid on homestead

    Honorably discharged veterans with a 90-100% combined VA disability rating may claim a refundable state income tax credit equal to the property tax timely paid on their West Virginia homestead (single-family residence used/occupied exclusively for residential purposes). The credit is claimed on the state income tax return. First available on 2024 returns filed in 2025. Property taxes must be paid on time (first half by Oct 1, second half by Apr 1).

Income Tax

  1. Military Retirement Income ExemptionAll veterans
    Military retirement fully tax-exempt (no cap)

    All military retirement income (regular Armed Forces, Reserves, National Guard, and uniformed services such as PHS and NOAA), including survivorship annuities, is fully exempt from West Virginia personal income tax as a decreasing modification to federal AGI for tax years beginning after Dec 31, 2017/2018. The earlier $20,000/$30,000 caps were repealed; it is now 100% exempt.

    Official details ↗
  2. VA Disability Compensation Not TaxedAll veterans
    Not taxable

    VA disability compensation is not subject to West Virginia income tax. West Virginia uses federal adjusted gross income as its starting point, and VA disability compensation is excluded from federal gross income, so it is never taxed at the state level. No special state modification is needed.

    Official details ↗

Education

  1. In-State Tuition for GI Bill RecipientsAll veterans
    In-state tuition rate

    All West Virginia public higher education institutions charge the in-state tuition rate to nonresident GI Bill recipients who enroll within three years of discharge (effective July 2015). Institutions also participate in the VA Yellow Ribbon Program.

    Official details ↗
  2. Medal of Honor / Purple Heart Tuition WaiverCombat service
    Full tuition and mandatory fee waiver

    All West Virginia public institutions of higher education waive tuition and mandatory fee charges for WV resident veterans who were awarded the Medal of Honor or the Purple Heart.

    Official details ↗
  3. Veterans Re-education Assistance (Education Stipend)All veterans
    Up to $500 per semester

    State-funded program providing up to a $500 per semester stipend to West Virginia veterans enrolled in a certified post-secondary education course. May also assist with vocational and non-traditional education on a case-by-case basis. Administered through the WV Department of Veterans Assistance.

    Official details ↗
  4. War Orphan Education ProgramAll veterans
    Tuition/fee waiver plus up to $1,000/semester ($2,000/year)

    Children (under age 26) and surviving spouses of qualifying deceased/disabled veterans receive a waiver of tuition and fees at West Virginia state post-secondary institutions, plus up to $1,000 per semester (maximum $2,000 per year) for educational expenses.

    Official details ↗

Employment

  1. Veterans Preference Points (State Civil Service)All veterans
    5 points (veteran) / 10 points (disabled veteran)

    Under W. Va. Code §6-13, qualifying veterans receive a 5-point preference added to a passing state employment examination score. Disabled veterans (meeting the 'disabled veteran' definition / compensable service-connected disability) receive a 10-point preference. DD-214 documentation required. Administered by the WV Division of Personnel.

    Official details ↗

Vehicle

  1. Disabled Veteran / Special Veteran License Plates100% P&T
    Free for 100% P&T / VA auto grant; $10 surcharge for general veteran plates

    Special license plates are available at no fee to veterans with a 100% permanent and total service-connected disability rating or who qualify for the federal VA automobile grant, as well as to former POWs, Purple Heart recipients, and Pearl Harbor survivors. Other honorably discharged veterans may obtain a veteran plate for $10 over the regular registration fee.

    Official details ↗

Recreation

  1. Free Hunting and Fishing License100% P&T
    Free hunting/fishing license

    Free hunting and fishing licenses for West Virginia resident veterans with a 100% permanent and total disability rating, those who qualify for the federal VA automobile grant, and former POWs. Active duty service members on leave/furlough may hunt and fish without a license.

    Official details ↗
  2. State Parks Lodging/Camping DiscountAll veterans
    10% discount on lodge rooms, cabins, and campsites

    Veterans and active-duty military receive a 10% discount on all standard lodge rooms, cabins, and campsites at West Virginia state parks, year-round.

    Official details ↗

Veterans Home

  1. West Virginia Veterans Home (Barboursville)All veterans
    125-bed residential/nursing/rehab facility

    State-operated 125-bed facility in Barboursville providing residential, nursing, and rehabilitative care to elderly and chronically ill West Virginia veterans and eligible family members. Authorized by the Legislature in 1975, opened 1981. Operated by the WV Department of Veterans Assistance.
