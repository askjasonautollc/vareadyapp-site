Georgia Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, Georgia offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, education, vehicle, recreation. Here's what Georgia veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. Homestead Tax Exemption100%+ rating
    Est. $3,800/yr · Up to $126,526 (2026) deducted from assessed value; typical savings ~$3,000–$4,500/yr depending on millage

    Disabled Veteran Homestead Exemption — exempts up to $126,526 (2026 amount, indexed annually) of the homestead's ASSESSED value from all ad valorem property taxes (state, county, municipal, school). Georgia assessed value = 40% of fair market value, so $126,526 of assessed value shields about $316,315 of home value. The portion above that remains taxable — it is NOT a full property tax exemption. Eligible: 100% service-connected disabled veterans, vets compensated at 100% for individual unemployability (TDIU), or vets with specific severe disabilities (loss of use of limbs, blindness, etc.). Unremarried surviving spouse and minor children also qualify. File at the local county tax commissioner with VA disability documentation, typically by April 1 of the tax year.

    How to claim: File at local county tax commissioner with VA disability award letter; deadline typically April 1

Education

  1. HERO ScholarshipAll veterans
    Est. $2,000/yr · Up to $2,000/yr × 4 yrs = $8,000 lifetime max

    Georgia HERO (Helping Educate Reservists and their Offspring) Scholarship. Despite the existing description, this is NOT a scholarship for dependents of disabled vets generally — it is specifically for: (1) active members of the GA National Guard or US Military Reserves who deployed for 181+ cumulative days in a designated combat zone since Feb 1, 2003 (or less if evacuated due to severe injuries); (2) the spouse of a qualifying member who was killed or disabled in combat; (3) dependent children of qualifying members, born before the deployment, age 25 or younger. Award: up to $2,000 per academic year, max 4 academic years ($8,000 lifetime). Children earn one award year per 181 days of qualifying combat service by the parent. Apply via Georgia Student Finance Commission.

    How to claim: Apply at gafutures.org via the Georgia Student Finance Commission with proof of combat-zone service

    Georgia Student Finance Commission · Official details ↗

Vehicle

  1. Free License Plate100%+ rating
    Est. $400/yr · Plate fees + 1 vehicle worth of ad valorem/TAVT — typically $200-$600/yr

    Free Disabled Veteran (DV) license plate for Georgia veterans with a 100% service-connected disability rating OR rated individually unemployable and paid at the 100% rate (TDIU). Veterans may receive up to 2 free DV plates for personal-use vehicles (cars or motorcycles). The first vehicle bearing the DV plate is also exempt from ad valorem and TAVT (title ad valorem tax). Purple Heart recipients qualify for a separate (also free) Purple Heart plate with the same tax benefits — Purple Heart eligibility requires only the PH certificate or DD-214 PH designation, NOT a disability rating.

    How to claim: Apply at county tag office with VA award letter showing 100% rating (or TDIU paid at 100% rate); Purple Heart applicants bring DD-214 or PH certificate

    Georgia Department of Revenue / County Tag Office · Official details ↗

Recreation

  1. Hunting/Fishing LicensesAll veterans
    Est. $170/yr · One-Time license is free (~$170 value); Disability license is steeply discounted over 3 years

    Two separate Georgia veteran hunting/fishing benefits. (1) FREE One-Time Veterans License — full sportsman's license (hunting, big game, migratory bird stamp, salt and freshwater fishing, mountain trout) valid for 1 year. Available to Georgia-resident veterans who served 90+ days active federal duty and were honorably discharged on or after July 1, 2005. NO disability rating required, but can only be issued once per veteran. (2) Disability Sportsman's License — 3-year heavily discounted (NOT free) license for veterans with a permanent and total (100% P&T) disability rating from the VA. Veterans with a mental impairment disability are eligible for a free honorary fishing license only. Both licenses require mailing the application with documentation to GA DNR — cannot be obtained or renewed online.

    How to claim: Mail application + DD-214 (One-Time) or VA P&T letter (Disability) to GA DNR. Call 800-366-2661.

    GA Dept of Natural Resources · Official details ↗
