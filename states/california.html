California Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, California offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, income tax, education, vehicle, recreation. Here's what California veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. Disabled Veteran Property Tax Exemption100%+ rating
    Est. $2,400/yr · Up to $271,009 of assessed value exempt — typical savings $1,800–$3,000/yr

    California Disabled Veterans' Exemption — for 2026, exempts up to $180,671 (Basic) or $271,009 (Low-Income) of the home's assessed value from property tax on the primary residence. Eligibility: veteran must be rated 100% service-connected disabled OR compensated at the 100% rate due to TDIU/individual unemployability. Low-Income tier requires household income at or below $81,131 in 2026 (income includes VA disability compensation). NOT a full exemption — value above the exempt amount remains taxable at the local rate (typically 1.0–1.25%). Unremarried surviving spouse may also qualify. Apply at County Assessor.

    How to claim: Apply at County Assessor with VA letter showing 100% rating or TDIU; income documentation for Low-Income tier

Education

  1. College Fee Waiver10%+ rating
    Est. $12,000/yr · Tuition/fees waived

    CalVet fee waiver at CA community colleges, CSU, and UC for eligible disabled veterans and dependents.

    How to claim: Apply through school financial aid office

    CalVet

Vehicle

  1. Disabled Veteran License Plate100%+ rating
    Est. $350/yr · Plate + full registration fee waived ($200–$800/yr depending on vehicle)

    California Disabled Veteran License Plates and Fee Waiver — DV plates issued free of charge, AND vehicle registration + license fees fully WAIVED for one passenger vehicle, motorcycle, or commercial vehicle with unladen weight ≤8,000 lbs. AB-390 (effective 2026): replacement DV plates also free. Eligibility: veteran must have a SINGLE condition rated 100% by VA or military service (combined ratings totaling 100% do NOT qualify) — must be a diagnosed disease/disorder that substantially impairs mobility. Surviving unremarried spouse may continue benefit.

    How to claim: Apply at CA DMV with VA letter showing single 100% condition rating

    CA DMV / CalVet · Official details ↗

Eligibility varies — some benefits require a VA disability rating, 100% P&T status, or combat service.
