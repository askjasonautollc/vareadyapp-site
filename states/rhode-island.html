Rhode Island Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, Rhode Island offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, income tax, education, employment, vehicle, recreation, veterans home. Here's what Rhode Island veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. Veterans' property tax exemption (RI Gen. Laws sec. 44-3-4)All veterans
    $1,000 base standard veteran; $10,000 for VA-rated totally disabled (service-connected); $10,000 specially adapted housing - municipalities may raise amounts by ordinance

    State statute sets a base exemption of $1,000 of assessed value for qualifying veterans (honorable discharge), with a $10,000 exemption for veterans the VA determines totally disabled through service-connected disability, and a $10,000 exemption for specially adapted housing. These are statutory minimums; each city/town may increase the dollar amounts (and partial-disability amounts) by local ordinance, so the actual exemption varies widely by municipality. The totally-disabled tier requires a VA total-disability determination (not necessarily permanent).

Income Tax

  1. Military Service Pension Modification (military retirement subtraction)All veterans
    80% exempt for tax year 2026; 100% (fully exempt) from tax year 2027

    Rhode Island lets military retirees subtract a percentage of military service pension benefits (included in federal AGI) from RI taxable income, with no age requirement. The subtraction is phasing up 20 points/year: 60% for TY2025, 80% for TY2026, and 100% (fully exempt) starting TY2027. — VERIFIED: Military service pensions are FULLY EXEMPT (100%) from Rhode Island Personal Income Tax for all tax years beginning on or after January 1, 2023 - there is NO phase-in. Under R.I. Gen. Laws 44-30-12(c)(11), a taxpayer subtracts the full amount of military service pension benefits included in federal AGI via Schedule M, with no age or income requirement. The research's claim of an 80%/20-points-per-year phase-in (80% for 2026, 100% in 2027) is incorrect; the official RI Division of Taxation Retirement Income Guide (Pub 2026-01, issued Feb 2026, Section 2) states military service pensions are fully exempt since TY2023. (Separately, VA disability compensation is also not taxed because RI starts from federal AGI, which excludes it.)

    Official details ↗
  2. VA disability compensation - not taxedAll veterans
    Not taxed by Rhode Island

    VA disability compensation is excluded from federal adjusted gross income, and Rhode Island's personal income tax starts from federal AGI, so VA disability compensation is not subject to RI state income tax. RI's military tax page addresses combat-zone pay exclusion/conformity but does not add VA disability comp back to income.

Education

  1. Disabled Veterans Tuition Waiver10%+ rating
    Full tuition waiver at RI public colleges/universities

    Free tuition at Rhode Island's public colleges and universities (CCRI, Rhode Island College, URI) for permanent RI-resident veterans with a 10-100% service-connected disability. Student must apply for and use available financial aid first.

    Official details ↗
  2. State Tuition Assistance ProgramAll veterans
    Up to 5 tuition-free classes per semester at RI public colleges

    Veterans may take up to five tuition-free classes per fall or spring semester at Rhode Island's public colleges and universities through the state tuition assistance program. Separately, active-duty members and their spouses/dependents get in-state tuition rates at CCRI, RIC, and URI regardless of residency.

    Official details ↗

Employment

  1. Veterans' hiring preference (civil service points)All veterans
    5 points (veteran) / 10 points (disabled veteran) on civil service exams

    Under RI Gen. Laws 36-4-19, veterans who pass a competitive state civil service entrance exam receive a 5-point credit added to their score; disabled veterans (war veteran with a VA service-connected disability) receive a 10-point credit. Applicant must achieve a passing score before the preference is applied.

    Official details ↗

Vehicle

  1. Disabled Veteran license plate / registration fee waiver100%+ rating
    Free disabled veteran plate (no registration plate fee)

    Rhode Island issues a disabled veteran license plate at no cost. The DMV requires proof of service (DD-214 or equivalent) and a VA form indicating 100% disabled. The veteran designation can also be added free to an existing RI license or ID.

    Official details ↗
  2. Veteran Disability Parking Placard100%+ rating
    Disabled parking placard

    Veterans who are 100% service-connected disabled may obtain a veteran disability parking placard by submitting the Veteran Disability Parking Placard Application plus a VA war-related disabled declaration letter. Veterans below the 100% threshold may instead apply for a standard disability placard with physician certification.

    Official details ↗

Recreation

  1. Free hunting/fishing license for 100% disabled100% P&T
    Free permanent hunting/fishing license; saltwater license exemption

    Persons with a 100% permanent disability status (from VA or SSA) are issued a no-cost permanent RI freshwater fishing, hunting, or combo license through DEM, and are exempt from needing a saltwater fishing license (must carry the original VA decision document). Requires a current original VA letter on agency letterhead.

    Official details ↗

Veterans Home

  1. Rhode Island Veterans Home (Bristol)All veterans
    Income-based assessment; 208-bed facility

    State-operated 208-bed residential and skilled nursing care facility in Bristol for Rhode Island war veterans. Residents pay an assessment based on a percentage of their income for housing and care; it is not a Medicaid facility and no asset spend-down is required to qualify. Contact 401-253-8000.
