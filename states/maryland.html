Maryland Veterans Benefits

Beyond your federal VA disability compensation, Maryland offers its own benefits for veterans — property tax, income tax, education, employment, vehicle, recreation, veterans home. Here's what Maryland veterans can claim, who qualifies, and how to apply.

Property Tax

  1. Disabled Veteran Real Property Tax Exemption (100% P&T)100% P&T
    Full exemption from real property tax on the dwelling and surrounding yard

    Full exemption from state and local real property taxes on the dwelling house and surrounding yard (lot/curtilage) for veterans the VA has rated 100% service-connected, permanent AND total. The dwelling must be the veteran's principal residence. Disabled active-duty members may also qualify. Apply via SDAT (form AT3-45/disabled-veteran form). 100% P&T is mandatory — no partial exemption exists for lower ratings at the state level (some counties offer separate local credits).

    Official details ↗
  2. Surviving Spouse Property Tax Exemption100% P&T
    Full exemption on the dwelling, continued for the surviving spouse

    An un-remarried surviving spouse of a qualifying 100% P&T disabled veteran, or surviving spouse of a service member killed in the line of duty, may continue/receive the full dwelling property tax exemption if they own and reside in the dwelling.

    Official details ↗

Income Tax

  1. Military Retirement Income SubtractionAll veterans
    Up to $12,500 (under age 55); up to $20,000 (age 55+)

    Maryland lets eligible individuals subtract military retirement income (active duty, reserves, MD National Guard, PHS/NOAA/Coast & Geodetic Survey commissioned) from federal AGI before computing Maryland tax. The amount is age-tiered, not a full exemption. Cannot exceed actual retirement income received.

    Official details ↗
  2. VA Disability Compensation Not TaxedAll veterans
    Fully non-taxable

    VA disability compensation is not subject to Maryland income tax (it is excluded from federal AGI, which is Maryland's starting point). No income limit.

    Official details ↗

Education

  1. Edward T. Conroy Memorial ScholarshipAll veterans

    Provides aid for tuition and other educational expenses to qualifying veterans (including disabled and deceased/POW/MIA categories) and their family members attending Maryland institutions of higher learning. Administered by the Maryland Higher Education Commission (MHEC). The DVMF page does not publish a specific dollar value or single disability threshold (eligibility spans multiple veteran/dependent categories), so the exact award amount should be confirmed with MHEC.

    Official details ↗
  2. Veterans of the Afghanistan and Iraq Conflicts (VAIC) ScholarshipCombat service
    Up to 50% of tuition, mandatory fees, and room & board (USM benchmark)

    Financial assistance for U.S. armed forces personnel who served in the Afghanistan or Iraq conflicts and their son/daughter/stepchild/spouse attending a Maryland postsecondary institution. Per MHEC, the award may not exceed 50% of annual tuition, mandatory fees, and room & board of the highest-cost USM four-year public resident undergraduate (excluding UMGC and UMB). Exact current cap should be confirmed via MHEC.

    Official details ↗

Employment

  1. Certified Disabled Veteran Hiring Preference (non-competitive appointment)30%+ rating
    Non-competitive (direct) appointment eligibility

    Veterans with an honorable discharge and a 30% or greater service-connected disability rating may be eligible for appointment to certain Maryland state positions without competitive selection. Governed by MD State Personnel & Pensions Article 7-203.

    Official details ↗
  2. State Employment Veterans' Preference (5/10 points)All veterans
    Up to 10 points added on selection tests/rankings

    Eligible veterans receive credit (commonly cited as up to 10 additional points) on selection tests/rankings for Maryland state employment; in non-scored processes military service is a positive determining factor when qualifications are otherwise equal. Governed by MD State Personnel & Pensions Article 7-207.

    Official details ↗

Vehicle

  1. Disabled Veteran License Plates (free, registration waived)100% P&T
    Free plates + free registration (no fee) for 100%/TDIU P&T

    Veterans rated 100% disabled — or compensated at the 100% rate due to individual unemployability (TDIU), permanent and total — receive disabled veteran license plates free of charge with no registration fee. Plates available with or without the wheelchair symbol, for passenger cars, multi-purpose vehicles, and trucks one ton or less. Issued with permanent cards/stickers.

    Official details ↗

Recreation

  1. Free Lifetime Hunting/Fishing License (100% SC or former POW)100%+ rating
    Free lifetime hunting/fishing license

    Maryland residents certified by the VA as 100% service-connected disabled, or certified former prisoners of war, qualify for a complimentary lifetime hunting and/or fishing license (includes bow, muzzleloader, and furbearer stamps/permits; excludes the annual Migratory Game Bird Stamp). VA documentation required; issued at DNR Licensing & Registration Service Centers.

    Official details ↗
  2. State Park Day-Use Entrance Fee Waiver (all veterans)All veterans
    Day-use entrance fee waived for the veteran

    All applicable day-use entrance charges at Maryland State Parks are waived for all veterans of the uniformed services, resident or out-of-state. The waiver applies to the veteran (per-vehicle fees may apply where charged). An annual State Park & Trail Passport ($75 resident / $100 non-resident) is an alternative for whole-vehicle entry.

    Official details ↗

Veterans Home

  1. Charlotte Hall Veterans HomeAll veterans
    Assisted living and skilled nursing care for eligible veterans/spouses

    Maryland's state veterans home (assisted living and skilled nursing). Eligibility: honorable discharge with full-time active duty (DD214/WD AGO 53-55); Maryland resident; and either age 62+ OR deemed disabled by SSA or the VA (a service connection of 70% or higher qualifies a veteran under age 62). Residency rules apply (3 months for skilled nursing, 2 years for assisted living, or MD home of record). Eligible spouses may also be served.

    Official details ↗
